Martin Luther King Jr.

Martin Luther King Jr. was a leader of the Civil Rights Movement, known for his role in advancing civil rights through nonviolent protest and his powerful speeches.

  • Born: January 15, 1929
  • Notable for: "I Have a Dream" speech
  • Leader of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC)
  1. Led the March on Washington in 1963
  2. Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1964
  3. Assassinated in 1968

Rosa Parks

Rosa Parks became a symbol of the Civil Rights Movement after her refusal to give up her seat on a segregated bus, sparking the Montgomery Bus Boycott.

  • Born: February 4, 1913
  • Notable for: Montgomery Bus Boycott
  • Known as "The Mother of the Civil Rights Movement"
  1. Her arrest led to a 381-day boycott
  2. Played a key role in ending segregation on buses
  3. Honored with the Congressional Gold Medal

Barack Obama

Barack Obama made history as the first African American president of the United States, with a presidency focused on healthcare reform and social justice.

  • Born: August 4, 1961
  • Notable for: 44th President of the United States
  • First African American president
  1. Elected President in 2008 and re-elected in 2012
  2. Passed the Affordable Care Act
  3. Won the Nobel Peace Prize in 2009
